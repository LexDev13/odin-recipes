How to make lasagna

While homemade lasagna may have a few steps, each step is easy and I assure you it’s worth the time; the perfect Italian meal!

Classic lasagna is a baked pasta dish of layered comfort. Wide noodles are stacked with a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce (ragù) and a creamy, smooth béchamel. Each layer is finished with a generous dusting of Parmesan cheese.

Baked until golden and bubbling, the result is a perfect, sliceable square. It offers a harmonious blend of tender pasta, savory meat, rich cream sauce, and a deliciously crisp, cheesy top. It's a hearty, satisfying centerpiece meal.

Ingredients:

For the Meat Sauce

  • Ground Beef
  • Onion
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper

For the Cheese Layers:

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • Shredded Mozarella
  • Grated Parmesan

Instructions:

  1. Make the sauce: Brown the ground beef with onion and garlic. Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven: Set to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Layer: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, then more sauce, dollops of ricotta, and a sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  4. Repeat: Continue layering (noodles, sauce, cheeses) until ingredients are used, finishing with a final layer of sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  5. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10-15 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  6. Rest: Let it sit for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
